Report on the Independent Assessment of Water Quantity and Quality near the Yanacocha Mining District, Cajamarca, Peru


This report describes the objectives, methods, results and conclusions of an independent investigation of potential affects of mining operations on water quantity and quality in the Andes mountains of Northern Peru, approximately 15 kilometers north of the city of Cajamarca. The mine is operated by Minera Yanacocha S.R.L., a joint venture of Newmont Peru Limited, Compania de Minas Buenaventura, and the International Finance Corporation.

This water quantity and quality investigation was commissioned by la Mesa de Dialogo y Consenso CAO-Cajamarca (the Mesa). The Mesa is a voluntary group of stakeholders with an interest in the economic, social and environmental consequences of the operation of the Yanacocha mine. The Mesa includes participants from rural and urban communities, public and private institutions, and Minera Yanacocha.

The Mesa collectively agreed in October 2001 that an independent study should be completed to assess potential water quantity and quality changes related to mining activities. Stratus Consulting was contracted by the CAO in January 2002 to perform this independent water study.
